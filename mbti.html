00. Background

Our fascination with the intricacies of human personality and its impact on life choices, particularly in the realm of music preferences, drives this project. The Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI), with its 16 distinct personality types, offers a comprehensive framework to investigate the interplay between personality traits and musical tastes. We are captivated by the possibility of uncovering trends, such as specific personality types favoring certain music genres or the predictive power of music preferences on determining one's MBTI classification.

Expanding beyond mere personal interest, we recognize the social implications of our study. Understanding these correlations can foster a deeper empathy and appreciation for the diverse ways individuals experience and interact with music, influenced by their unique psychological make-up. This insight has the potential to enhance social bonding, improve mental health interventions through tailored music therapy, and offer innovative approaches in the music industry for targeted outreach and content creation. By blending scientific inquiry with artistic exploration, our project aims to contribute meaningfully to the broader discourse on the intersection of psychology, art, and social well-being.

Target Audience

1. General public with an interest in music and/or personality tests (Major Audience)
2. Music and entertainment industry professionals seeking insights for them to better target their audience/users
3. Psychologists and therapists interested in public perception in personality tests

01. Data Collection & Test

DataSet

The data we use contains the results of a survey that explores the relationship between MBTI and music preferences. Hosted on Reddit's r/SampleSize community and compiled into a Google spreadsheet, the survey garnered around 500 responses. The dataset is rich and multifaceted, including a range of categorical, ordinal, and derived fields.
Before we reach the final design, we did a quick test to see if the data is suitable for visualization and define the storyboard narrative we want to present.

02. Custom Figure

MBTI Avatars

The Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) is a personality framework that categorizes individuals into 16 distinct types based on preferences in four areas: Introversion/Extraversion, Sensing/Intuition, Thinking/Feeling, and Judging/Perceiving. It's widely used for personal growth, career planning, and team building.

In our project, we've transformed traditional MBTI avatars into new, adorable, doodle-like versions . This creative approach aims to enhance interaction and enjoyment, making the experience more engaging and game-like. It's particularly fitting for our unique website that deciphers your personality through your music preferences, offering a fun and immersive way to explore the fascinating world of MBTI.
